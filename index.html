About Me

I am a ICU dietitian with 6+ years of experience translating research and evidence-based practice into clinical guidelines and institutional policy. Pivoting into data science, I bring systems level thinking and a record of delivering measurable outcomes in dynamic environments.

Key accomplishments include:
  • Establishing ICU nutrition care guidelines at the onset of the COVID pandemic well ahead of publications from expert groups (later found to be in-line with our guidance)
  • Adding copper to hospital formulary after identifying a population at high risk of deficiency (CRRT patients)
  • Delivering annual lectures to surgical and medical residents, aligning frontline practice with evolving clinical evidence

While healthcare has been my professional foundation, I’ve always wanted to expand my scope—with academic interests spanning chemistry, math, linguistics, East Asian studies, and policy. I thrive in fast-paced, intellectually demanding environments and enjoy turning complex data into actionable insight.

To formalize my pivot, I'm completing the TripleTen Data Science Bootcamp, gaining practical experience with Python, SQL, machine learning, and data visualization. I’m also strengthening my quantitative skills through applied calculus and advanced statistics coursework.

I’m excited to apply my analytical and communication strengths in consulting, where I can help organizations solve complex, data-driven problems across sectors—whether in healthcare, sustainability, education, or emerging tech.

Outside of work, I’m a 4x marathon runner (currently on pause), an avid home cook, and a Pilates enthusiast.
